The Incongruity Theory

The Incongruity Theory states that we live in an orderly world and when something occurs that does not fit, our expectations are exceeded and we laugh. John Morreall suggests that combining the general ideas of the three theories produces this statement: laughter is “a boost in positive feelings, a cessation of negative feelings, or the release of the suppressed feelings." From these ideas, Morreall creates one singular formula: “laughter results from a pleasant psychological shift." Morreall directly challenges Plato’s argument by stating that laughter is under our control. The muscles involved in laughter are purely voluntary; therefore his statement fits all situations.
