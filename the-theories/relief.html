The Relief Theory

The Relief Theory was created in the 1700’s, which was the idea that laughter releases animal spirits that have been building up in one’s body. This idea was later supported by the advancement in biology and more modern thinks such as Herbert Spencer and Sigmund Freud.

The easiest way to think about this theory is that laughing releases energy. Spencer says this occurs when some emotion has built up but it is not appropriate to express it, laughter is the way to release this pent up emotion.

Fraud describes this idea in three categories the “jokes,” the “comic” and “humor." The joke is the idea that we store the energy in our bodies that would normally be suppressed feelings. In reacting to the comic, we save energy in thought and in humor we save emotionally energy. The problem with this is that not every joke we react to suggests a pent up or repressed emotion.
