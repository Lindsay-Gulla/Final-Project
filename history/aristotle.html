Aristotle

Although this was a much-accepted belief at this time, one might question a jesters place in all of this. A jester is generally hired by a king to be a professional joker to entertain him.

Aristotle writes about this idea in Nicomachean Ethics. Much of Aristotle’s take on humor coincides with Plato’s idea that humor is malicious. He wrote that, “Most people enjoy amusement and jesting more than they should…a jest is a kind of mockery, and lawgivers forbid some kinds of mockery- perhaps they ought to have forbidden some kinds of jesting."

Although he is not directly talking about jesters, this shows the association to them would be mockery. Therefore, the fact that certain people are appointed to make a mockery of oneself makes sense with the times; there were designated people to laugh at. Jesters were the time and place for humor in past societies.
