Thomas Hobbes and Rene Descartes

The 18th century brought more negativity about humor with two philosophers, Thomas Hobbes and Rene Descartes. Hobbes argued that laughter is a sign that one believes they are superior to another. He says that laughter expresses “a sudden glory arising from some conception of some eminency in ourselves, by comparison with the infirmity of others, or with our formerly” (Morreall, 2015, pp. 244). Descartes writes that humor accompanies three of the six basic emotions- wonder, love, mild hatred, desire, joy, and sadness. These concepts lead to the 20th century discovery of “The Superiority Theory.”
