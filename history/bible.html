Early Christian Thinkers

This negative connotation of humor stems from the bible and early Christian thinkers. Humor in the bible is associated with hostility. There are many references to God laughing with hostility, for example, “The kings of the earth stand ready, and the rulers conspire together against the lord and his anointed kingâ€¦ The Lord who sits enthroned in heaven laughs them to scorn; then he rebukes them in anger, he threatens them in his wrath” (Psalm 2:2-5).

The bible is explicitly against mockery and it is considered offensive and punishable to death. Christian thinkers pushed a serious and self-controlled life, which excludes laughter. Laughter cannot always be explained in that it can happen randomly and your body can shake or react differently. This was seen as a lack of control over one’s life.

Laughter was associated with foul language and it was argued foul discourse would be followed by foul actions. This distaste for humor went so far for some, such as the Puritans when they took rule in England in the 17th century, they outlawed comedies.
