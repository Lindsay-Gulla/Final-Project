Jean Harvey

These types of offensive jokes coincide with Jean Harvey’s argument. The basis of her argument is in opposition with John Morreall’s, she argues that humor is dangerous. Harvey believes that humor is a lens for negativity, highlights negative stereotypes, and is socially harmful, (Tapley, 2006, pp. 421).

She even goes so far in saying that humor has no social value and only those who have social power can enjoy humor positively. Morreall claims that humor gives people perspective on their life’s accomplishments, failures, and general outlook, which allows for a balanced ego and general sense of well-being. He argues that seeing the humor in a serious situation allows people to relieve themselves of stress and anxiety. In other words if you don’t laugh you cry. Harvey disputes these claims completely.

She argues that become humor is social; this does not fit Morreall’s analysis because it applies to the individual. She believes Morreall’s approach is “morally flawed,” because “he irresponsibly ignores the inherent moral implications of the social nature of humor,” (Tapely, 2006, pp. 423).
