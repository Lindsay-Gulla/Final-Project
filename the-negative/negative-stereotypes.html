Negative Stereotypes

One of the main arguments against humor is that it perpetuates stereotypes.

As Harvey suggests, humor is a social act, one would not laugh or joke by themselves; therefore it occurs in groups of mixed social status. She believes that joking strengthens the power of the individuals with higher social status.

Harvey claims that the butt of the joke tends to be those of lower social status. The joke teller is usually part of the group in power and by others laughing at the joke, they reinforcing this person’s skewed perception. There are other examples that can be found in Family Guy that proves Harvey’s point.
