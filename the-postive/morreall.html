John Morreall

John Morreall’s theory makes sense with Kuipers’ analysis. He writes that a psychological shift occurs during this; a person goes from a state of serious thinking to observing something abnormal, which breaks our normal thinking patterns and we turn to a state of amusement (incongruity theory). He stresses that in order to laugh we must be “caught off guard” for this psychological shift to occur suddenly (Morreall, 2015, pp. 248).
