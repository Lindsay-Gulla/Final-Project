Compare to Family Guy

In order to test Kuipers’ theory, it is important to look at the types of jokes that are generated today about the disaster of 9/11. To do so, I will analyze different clips of Family Guy. Family Guy is a satirical based show that features an American family, The Griffins who represent different types of Americans.

Peter Griffin, the father, is portrayed as an idiot who is always into a different issue or event each episode. Lois, his wife, is smarter than Peter; however, is a housewife that represses much of her husband’s actions. Meg Griffin, the daughter in the family, is the punch-line to every joke; that person that always gets picked on from school to home. Chris Griffin is the oldest son; he is like his father in that he is portrayed to be extremely stupid. The baby, Stewie and the dog, Brian are seemingly the smartest in the family. Brian is a liberal, atheist, who is very opinionated, while Stewie as an infant acts as an evil scientist, constantly, building time machines or other gadgets.

Although I would argue that Family Guy’s brand of humor pokes fun at the way American’s think, some jokes have been taken to be very offensive. It is interesting to examine the treatment of 9/11 jokes in the show to analyze which were found offensive and which were found amusing.
