9/11's Affect on Humor

 

Humor can work as a coping mechanism for a marginalized group of people. An example of a time in recent history where the United States was extremely united is the tragic events that took place September 11, 2001. This was a time where Americans had believed that this event was “the death of comedy” (Kuipers, 2011, pp. 20). One might question, how this event went from the death of comedy to the subject of many jokes on American-based websites.

Giselinde Kuipers arrived in the United States a year after 9/11 occurred to examine how the tragic event changed comedy in the U.S. She breaks it down into three elapses of time, immediately following the event there was “a suspension of humor,” followed by “the call for humor as a means to cope with the events of 9/11,” and lastly the “jokes that did emerge about these events, notwithstanding the public discourse about the inappropriateness of such humor” (Kuipers 2011, pp. 20).

Kuipers defines disaster jokes as “sick jokes, based on an incongruity between the gruesome and the innocuous” (Kuipers, 2011, pp. 21). Kuipers believes that the basis of disaster jokes are usually combining a humorous topic a reference to something shocking or taboo, or combining the serious event with unserious events or topics. "Time puts a psychological distance between us and the event, thereby creating the ideal balance of threat and safety in the recipe for humor."
