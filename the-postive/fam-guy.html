9/11 Humor

If an American were asked to describe what 9/11 means, they would do so with patriotism and defense for their country, they would describe the imagery, what they saw and how they felt. Many Americans would not be able to explain why it happened, who caused it, and other details. “A Knight-Ridder poll in January 2003 found that 44% of respondents thought that most or all of the hijackers on 9/11 were Iraqi; in fact, none were” (Mahan and Griset, 2013, pp. 217). This display of ignorance was the set up for a joke in Family Guy, which proves a point about America’s knowledge of an event that so horribly changed life. Here is a conversation between Peter and Brian:
Peter Griffin: Ground Zero. So this is where the first guy got AIDS.
Brian Griffin: Peter, this is the site of the 9/11 terrorist attacks.
Peter Griffin: Oh so Saddam Hussein did this?
Brian Griffin: No.
Peter Griffin: The Iraqi army?
Brian Griffin: No.
Peter Griffin: Some guys from Iraq?
Brian Griffin: No.
Peter Griffin: That one lady who visited Iraq that one time?
Brian Griffin: No, Peter Iraq had nothing to do with this, it was a bunch of Saudi Arabians, Lebanese, and Egyptians financed by a Saudi Arabian guy living in Afghanistan and sheltered by Pakistanis.
Peter Griffin: So you're saying we need to invade Iran?
(Source: Macfarlane, 2008, “Baby Not on Board”)

This episode aired seven years after the tragic event in 2008. As an American, I do not find this joke offensive. This joke would fall under the Relief Theory. This shows McFarlane’s frustration with American ignorance. Therefore, this is an exaggeration of a perception of American thought, by poking fun at it; McFarlane is able to criticize these types of people through a satirical lens. He is releasing suppressed or pent up feelings about ignorant or uneducated Americans. I would argue this makes fun of the aftermath thinking of 9/11, not the actual tragedy.
